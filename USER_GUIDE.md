## 🎯 Welcome to the HMDA Stakeholder Dashboard

This interactive tool helps you explore mortgage lending data in your community. Whether you're a community organizer, researcher, policymaker, or concerned resident, this dashboard makes complex housing finance data accessible and understandable.

## 📊 Understanding the Data

### What is HMDA?
The **Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA)** requires most mortgage lenders to report their lending patterns. This includes:
- Who applies for mortgages
- Who gets approved or denied
- Loan amounts and terms
- Applicant demographics

### Data Years Available
- **2019-2024**: Six years of comprehensive data
- **Most Recent**: 2024 data (latest available)
- **Updated**: Annually when new data is released

### What the Numbers Mean

#### Approval Rates
- **Good range**: 70-85% (normal market conditions)
- **Below 60%**: May indicate restrictive lending
- **Above 90%**: May indicate lax standards

#### Loan Amounts
- **National average**: ~$250,000 (varies by location)
- **Regional differences**: Higher in expensive housing markets
- **Trends**: Track changes over time

#### Denial Reasons
Common reasons for denial:
- Debt-to-income ratio too high
- Credit history issues
- Insufficient collateral

## 🏠 Understanding Your Community

### Interpreting Approval Rates

#### High Approval Rates (80%+)
- May indicate: Strong lending market
- Could mean: Creditworthy applicants, good economy
- Watch for: Potential for predatory lending

#### Low Approval Rates (Under 60%)
- May indicate: Restrictive lending standards
- Could mean: Economic challenges, credit issues
- Action needed: Financial education programs

#### Demographic Differences
- **Small differences** (1-3%): Normal variation
- **Moderate differences** (4-10%): Worth investigating
- **Large differences** (10%+): Potential fair lending concerns

### Regional Context
Remember that lending patterns vary by:
- **Housing costs**: Expensive areas have larger loans
- **Economic conditions**: Job growth affects lending
- **Population density**: Urban vs. rural patterns

## ⚠️ Important Limitations

### What HMDA Doesn't Tell Us
- **Credit scores**: Not included in public data
- **Detailed financial information**: Privacy protected
- **Individual decisions**: Aggregated data only
- **Why someone was denied**: Only general categories

### Data Quality Notes
- **Self-reported**: Provided by lenders
- **May have errors**: Lenders can make reporting mistakes
- **Missing data**: Some fields not always completed
- **Time lags**: Data takes time to compile and release

### Interpretation Guidelines
- **Correlation vs. causation**: Approval differences don't prove discrimination
- **Multiple factors**: Many reasons for lending patterns
- **Context matters**: Consider local economic conditions
- **Professional advice**: Consult experts for serious concerns
