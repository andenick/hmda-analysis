What is HMDA Data?

The Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA) requires banks and lenders to report information about mortgage applications and loans. This helps communities:

  • Understand lending patterns - See where loans are being made and who is getting them
  • Identify discrimination - Spot unfair lending practices based on race, ethnicity, or geography
  • Hold lenders accountable - Know which institutions are serving your community well
  • Advocate for change - Use data to support fair housing initiatives

Is There Discrimination?

Examining approval rates by race and ethnicity

Understanding Disparities

Lending disparities occur when people from different racial or ethnic groups are approved for loans at different rates, even when they have similar qualifications.

Why it matters: Fair housing laws prohibit discrimination in lending. If you see significant differences in approval rates, it may indicate discriminatory practices that deserve investigation.

What You Can Do

If you believe you've experienced discrimination in lending:

  • File a complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
  • Contact HUD Fair Housing at 1-800-669-9777
  • Reach out to local fair housing organizations in your area
